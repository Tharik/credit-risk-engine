# What worked well

* Accelerated scaffolding
* Reduced boilerplate time
* Improved test breadth
* Helped refine documentation quality
* Improved delivery speed under aggressive timeline

---

# What required careful oversight

* Some framework version mismatches (.NET 10 vs .NET 8)
* Enum deserialization issues
* Docker compatibility issues
* Overly abstract suggestions in early design phases

---

# Key lesson

AI was most effective when:

* Used as accelerator
* Challenged critically
* Combined with engineering judgment
* Validated against requirements

---

# What I'd do differently next time

* Use AI earlier for CI/CD templates
* Prompt more aggressively for edge cases upfront
* Validate framework compatibility sooner
* Standardize prompt iteration faster

---

# Final Reflection

AI significantly improved development velocity, but architectural quality still depended entirely on:

* Human validation
* Design restraint
* Strategic simplification
* Technical leadership

The best outcomes came from combining AI speed with deliberate engineering judgment.
